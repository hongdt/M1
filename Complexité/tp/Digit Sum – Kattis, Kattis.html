Digit Sum

For a pair of integers aa and bb, the digit sum of the interval [a,b][a,b] is defined as the sum of all digits occurring in all numbers between (and including) aa and bb. For example, the digit sum of [28,31][28,31] can be calculated as:

2+8+2+9+3+0+3+1=282+8+2+9+3+0+3+1=28

Given the numbers aa and bb, calculate the digit sum of [a,b][a,b].

Input

On the first line one positive number: the number of test cases, at most 100. After that per test case:

  • one line with two space-separated integers, aa and bb (0≤a≤b≤1015)(0≤a≤b≤1015).

Output

Per test case:

  • one line with an integer: the digit sum of [a,b][a,b].

0 10
28 31
